Outreach

It's a common misconception that mathematical ability is an innate, genetic trait--as if it were a personality characteristic you’re either born with or without. In reality, this belief becomes a self-fulfilling prophecy. When individuals internalize the idea that they lack inherent aptitude, they are less likely to invest sustained effort, thereby constraining their own development.

I was no exception to this mindset. In high school, math was consistently my most challenging subject, which led me to believe I lacked the natural ability required for fields like astrophysics. As a result, when I entered community college, I deliberately avoided math-heavy paths, choosing majors like business instead. However, my passion for astronomy couldn't be ignored, and I finally leaned into the challenge of building my mathematical foundation in order to pursue a career in astrophysics.

When I began sharing my journey on the social media platform TikTok, I began to receieve messages from other yound students who dreamed of becoming scientists but were held back by the same mindset I once had. This is precisely why science communication and outreach has become so important to me, because representation matters. Seeing people succeed in STEM who look like them or share similar backgrounds reinforces the message that they belong in that space too. As science communication continues to evolve online, my hope is that it will bring fresh perspectives into STEM, allowing the next generation to drive progress in directions we have yet to conceive.
