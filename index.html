About Me

Born and raised in Cincinnati, Ohio, I began my academic journey at Cincinnati State Technical and Community College before earning a Bachelor of Science in Astrophysics from the University of Cincinnati in May 2025. Following my undergraduate studies, I completed post-baccalaureate coursework at the University of Colorado Boulder to further strengthen my theoretical foundation in preparation for graduate study. I intend to pursue a Ph.D. in Astrophysics, with a research focus broadly centered on cosmology. More specifically, Iâ€™m interested in the role of dark matter in driving galaxy formation and large-scale structure, particularly using hydrodynamical simulations.

Outside of research, I enjoy hiking and skiing (especially since having moved to Colorado), as well as volunteering my time at the Chamberlin Observatory near downtown Denver, where I can indulge in my passion for astronomy and stay engaged with the community. I am also deeply committed to science communication. As an intern and program educator at the Cincinnati Observatory, I developed educational programs and outreach initiatives to make astronomy more accessible to the public. I also create STEM content on the social media platform TikTok, sharing my nontraditional path to astrophysics in hopes of inspiring the next generation of young scientists. More details on my science communication efforts can be found in the Outreach section.
